**Case 1:**

A 45-year-old woman presents to her primary care physician with complaints of persistent fatigue and dry eyes for the past few months. She also mentions that she has been experiencing an itch all over her body, with no rash. She has a history of autoimmune thyroid disease and her mother was diagnosed with PBC.

*What is the most likely diagnosis of the patient?*

The most likely diagnosis for this patient is **Primary Biliary Cirrhosis (PBC)**. Her symptoms of fatigue and itchy skin, along with dry eyes, are common initial manifestations of PBC. Her personal history of an autoimmune disease (thyroid disease) and family history of PBC increase her risk.
