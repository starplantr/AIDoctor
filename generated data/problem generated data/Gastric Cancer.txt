**Case 1:**

A 65-year-old man presents with vague abdominal pain that has been gradually worsening over the past few months. He also mentions unintended weight loss and a decreased appetite. He notes that he feels full quickly after beginning meals and his wife has noticed that he seems more fatigued lately. He has a long-standing history of smoking and enjoys salty, smoked foods. He mentions that he had an infection with H. pylori several years ago that was treated with antibiotics.

*What is the most likely diagnosis of the patient?*

Given the patient's age, history of smoking, dietary habits, previous H. pylori infection, and symptoms of abdominal pain, early satiety, weight loss, and fatigue, the most likely diagnosis is **Gastric Cancer**.
