**Case 1:**

A 68-year-old Native American woman presents to her primary care physician with a 2-month history of intermittent right upper quadrant abdominal pain and noticeable weight loss. She states that she has also noticed her skin and eyes becoming slightly yellow over the past few weeks. Her past medical history is significant for chronic gallstones. Physical examination shows mild tenderness in the right upper quadrant of her abdomen and slight jaundice.

*What is the most likely diagnosis of the patient?*

The most likely diagnosis for this patient is **Gallbladder Cancer**. Her age, ethnicity (Native American), history of chronic gallstones, right upper quadrant pain, weight loss, and jaundice are all consistent with this diagnosis. Gallbladder cancer often presents late with non-specific symptoms, and it is more common in individuals with a history of gallstones and chronic gallbladder inflammation.
