**Case 1:**

A 35-year-old man arrives at your clinic with complaints of severe diarrhea, abdominal cramps, and nausea for the past two days. He admits to feeling feverish and generally unwell. He recently returned from a camping trip where he admits the sanitation conditions were less than ideal, and he drank water directly from a stream. 

*What is the most likely diagnosis of the patient?*

The patient's symptoms of diarrhea, abdominal cramps, nausea, and recent exposure to potentially contaminated water during his camping trip strongly suggest a diagnosis of **Gastroenteritis**, possibly due to a bacterial or parasitic infection.

**Case 2:**

A 6-year-old girl is brought to your office by her mother, who reports that her daughter has had multiple episodes of vomiting, diarrhea, and a low-grade fever for the past day. The mother mentions that there was a reported outbreak of gastroenteritis in her daughter's school. 

*What is the most likely diagnosis of the patient?*

Given the child's symptoms of vomiting, diarrhea, fever, and the reported outbreak of gastroenteritis in her school, the most likely diagnosis is **Gastroenteritis**, likely viral in origin.

**Case 3:**

A 60-year-old woman with a history of rheumatoid arthritis, for which she is on immunosuppressive therapy, presents to your clinic with severe diarrhea, nausea, and abdominal cramps. She also reports feeling extremely thirsty and appears to be disoriented.

*What is the most likely diagnosis of the patient?*

The patient's symptoms of severe diarrhea, nausea, abdominal cramps, and symptoms of dehydration, along with her history of immunosuppression, suggest a likely diagnosis of **Gastroenteritis**, potentially severe due to her weakened immune system.

**Case 7:**

A 45-year-old woman comes to your clinic complaining of fever, abdominal pain, and watery diarrhea. She tells you that she started a new antibiotic course prescribed by her dentist for a dental infection about a week ago.

*What is the most likely diagnosis of the patient?*

Considering the patient's symptoms and recent use of antibiotics, the most likely diagnosis is **Gastroenteritis**. More specifically, this may be a case of Clostridium difficile infection, which can occur following antibiotic treatment.
