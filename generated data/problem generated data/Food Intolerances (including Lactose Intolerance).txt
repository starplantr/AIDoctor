**Case 1:**

A 25-year-old Asian woman presents to the clinic complaining of bloating, diarrhea, and abdominal cramps that have persisted for a few weeks. She notes that the symptoms seem to be worse after she consumes milk or ice cream. She reports a similar pattern in her mother and her older sister. 

*What is the most likely diagnosis of the patient?*

This patient's symptoms of bloating, diarrhea, and abdominal cramps, which are worse after consuming lactose-containing products, along with her family history, suggest **lactose intolerance**. Lactose intolerance is common in people of East Asian descent due to decreased lactase activity, an enzyme needed to break down lactose, a sugar in milk.

**Case 2:**

A 45-year-old woman presents with a history of bloating and diarrhea that occur within a few hours after eating certain foods, particularly dried fruits and wine. She also has a history of mild asthma. 

*What is the most likely diagnosis of the patient?*

The patient's symptoms after consuming certain types of food, especially dried fruits and wine, along with her asthma, suggest a **food intolerance** related to sulfites. Sulfites are often used as preservatives in dried fruits, wine, and other foods, and can trigger asthma and gastrointestinal symptoms in sensitive individuals.

**Case 3:**

A 30-year-old man reports that he often feels bloated and has loose stools after eating. He cannot pinpoint it to any specific food but says that his symptoms are worse during stressful periods at work. 

*What is the most likely diagnosis of the patient?*

The patient's generalized symptoms after eating, which are worse during periods of stress, suggest a possible **food intolerance** that is likely worsened by psychological factors. Stress can influence the gut and exacerbate gastrointestinal symptoms, especially in those with food intolerances.

**Case 4:**

A 20-year-old college student complains of recurrent episodes of abdominal cramping and diarrhea, particularly following meals at the cafeteria. She noticed that her symptoms tend to worsen whenever she eats anything with wheat, rye, or barley.

*What is the most likely diagnosis of the patient?*

This patient's symptoms of abdominal cramping and diarrhea, especially after consuming wheat, rye, or barley products, suggest **celiac disease**. Celiac disease is an immune-mediated reaction to gluten, a protein found in wheat, rye, and barley. This diagnosis can be confirmed with a serological test for tissue transglutaminase antibodies and confirmed with duodenal biopsy.

**Case 5:**

He mentions that his symptoms seem to be worse after eating cheese and yogurt, and he also admits to having decreased his dairy intake due to lactose intolerance diagnosed in his 40s.

*What is the most likely diagnosis of the patient?*

The persistent discomfort and bowel irregularities in this patient, especially after consuming lactose-containing products like cheese and yogurt, in combination with his previous diagnosis, suggest a likely exacerbation of his known **lactose intolerance**. His age also fits the typical profile, as lactase activity can decrease with age, exacerbating symptoms in individuals with lactose intolerance.

She has a past medical history of Irritable Bowel Syndrome (IBS) and notes that these episodes are similar to her IBS flares, but seems to be getting worse over the past few months.

This patient's symptoms, combined with her past medical history of IBS, suggest the possibility of a **food intolerance**. Patients with IBS often have food intolerances that can exacerbate their IBS symptoms. A detailed dietary history and possibly an elimination diet may help identify specific triggers.

**Case 15:**

A 36-year-old man of East Asian descent presents with abdominal pain, gas, and diarrhea. He states that these symptoms occur shortly after consuming dairy products. He remembers his father used to have similar problems with dairy products as well.

*What is the most likely diagnosis of the patient?*

The patient's symptoms occurring after the consumption of dairy products, his East Asian descent, and a family history of similar symptoms, all point towards **lactose intolerance**. Lactase deficiency leading to lactose intolerance is particularly common among people of East Asian descent. The undigested lactose in the gut leads to osmotic diarrhea and fermentation by gut bacteria, causing the symptoms he's experiencing.
