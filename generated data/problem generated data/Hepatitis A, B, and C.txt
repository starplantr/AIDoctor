**Case 1:**

A 26-year-old man presents to the emergency department with complaints of fatigue, loss of appetite, and abdominal discomfort for the past two weeks. He has also noticed his urine has been unusually dark and his eyes appear yellow. He mentions that he recently traveled to a developing country where he consumed local street food.

**What is the most likely diagnosis of the patient?**

This patient likely has Hepatitis A. He presents with symptoms typical of hepatitis, including fatigue, anorexia, abdominal discomfort, dark urine, and jaundice. The recent history of travel to a developing country and consumption of street food suggest the possibility of contamination, leading to an infection with the Hepatitis A virus, which is transmitted through the fecal-oral route.

**Case 2:**

**What is the most likely diagnosis of the patient?**

The patient likely has Hepatitis B. Her symptoms of fever, fatigue, and jaundice are consistent with hepatitis. Given her profession and the history of a needlestick injury, she is at a high risk for Hepatitis B, which can be transmitted through contact with infected blood.
