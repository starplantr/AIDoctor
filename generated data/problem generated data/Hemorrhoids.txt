**Case 1:**

A 65-year-old male presents to your clinic complaining of painless bright red blood on the toilet paper after bowel movements for the past two weeks. He also reports a sensation of discomfort and swelling around his anus. He has a history of chronic constipation and admits that he often strains during bowel movements. He is overweight with a BMI of 31.

**What is the most likely diagnosis of the patient?**

The most likely diagnosis is hemorrhoids. His age, history of chronic constipation with straining during bowel movements, obesity, and the symptoms of painless rectal bleeding and discomfort in the anal region are all indicative of this condition.

**Case 2:**

A 28-year-old pregnant woman presents to your clinic with complaints of discomfort and a painful lump near her anus. She also mentions having spotted bright red blood on her stool a few times. She has been experiencing constipation, which she attributes to her pregnancy.

The most likely diagnosis is hemorrhoids. Pregnancy is a common risk factor for hemorrhoids due to increased pressure in the abdomen and hormonal changes. Constipation, a common issue during pregnancy, can further increase the risk. Her symptoms of a painful lump near the anus and spotting of blood on stool are also indicative of hemorrhoids.
