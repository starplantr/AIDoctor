**Case 1:**

A 36-year-old man presents to the clinic with complaints of fatigue, itching, and abdominal pain. He has also noticed a yellowing of his skin and eyes. He has a history of ulcerative colitis which was diagnosed 5 years ago.

*What is the most likely diagnosis of the patient?*

The most likely diagnosis for this patient is **Primary Sclerosing Cholangitis (PSC)**. His symptoms of fatigue, pruritus, jaundice, and abdominal pain, along with his history of ulcerative colitis, point toward PSC. PSC is often associated with inflammatory bowel disease, particularly ulcerative colitis.
