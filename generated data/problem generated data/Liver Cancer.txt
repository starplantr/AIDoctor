**Case 1:**

A 52-year-old man presents to the clinic with generalized fatigue and unexplained weight loss over the past few months. He also mentions a dull ache in the upper right quadrant of his abdomen. His medical history is significant for chronic hepatitis C infection which was treated 10 years ago.

*What is the most likely diagnosis of the patient?*

The patient's symptoms of fatigue, unexplained weight loss, and abdominal pain in the context of a history of chronic hepatitis C infection suggest a diagnosis of **liver cancer**. Chronic hepatitis C is a known risk factor for liver cancer.

**Case 2:**

A 65-year-old woman presents with worsening jaundice over the past two weeks. She also reports loss of appetite and feeling full after eating small meals. Her medical history is significant for cirrhosis due to chronic alcohol abuse.

*What is the most likely diagnosis of the patient?*

This patient's symptoms of jaundice, loss of appetite, early satiety, and a history of cirrhosis due to alcohol abuse are indicative of **liver cancer**. Cirrhosis, especially due to alcohol abuse, is a major risk factor for the development of liver cancer.
