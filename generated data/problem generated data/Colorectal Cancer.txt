**Case 1:**

A 58-year-old man presents with a 3-month history of changes in bowel habits. He reports alternating periods of constipation and diarrhea and has noticed blood on his toilet paper. The patient also reports unintentional weight loss of about 10 pounds over the same period. He is a heavy smoker and has a high-fat diet. His father was diagnosed with colorectal cancer at age 60.

*What is the most likely diagnosis of the patient?*

Given the patient's age, symptoms of changes in bowel habits, rectal bleeding, weight loss, smoking history, diet, and family history of colorectal cancer, the most likely diagnosis is **Colorectal Cancer**.
