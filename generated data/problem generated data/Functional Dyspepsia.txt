**Case 1:**

A 32-year-old woman presents to your clinic complaining of chronic upper abdominal discomfort. She describes the pain as a burning sensation that worsens when she eats. The discomfort is accompanied by early satiety and frequent belching. She has had these symptoms for almost 6 months now, and they seem to come and go. Her past medical history is unremarkable, but she does mention she often finds herself under high levels of stress due to her demanding job.

*What is the most likely diagnosis of the patient?*

The most likely diagnosis for this patient is **Functional Dyspepsia**. Her chronic upper abdominal discomfort, feeling of early fullness when eating, and frequent belching are all indicative of this condition. The fact that she has chronic stress, which can exacerbate symptoms, further supports this diagnosis.
