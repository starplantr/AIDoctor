**Case 1:**

A 60-year-old male with a long-standing history of arthritis for which he takes ibuprofen regularly, presents with a burning, gnawing pain in the middle of his abdomen that seems to wake him up at night. The pain appears to be relieved by eating, but it returns after an hour or so. He also mentions that he has been feeling more tired than usual recently.

**What is the most likely diagnosis of the patient?**

The patient's most likely diagnosis is peptic ulcer disease (PUD). His regular use of nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs) such as ibuprofen, combined with his typical symptoms of nocturnal and hunger-induced abdominal pain that is relieved by eating, are classic for PUD. The fatigue he's been feeling could be due to anemia, which is common in PUD due to chronic, slow bleeding from the ulcer.
