**Case 1:**

A 72-year-old woman presents to the clinic with the complaint of feeling a bulge through her anus for the past few weeks. She says the protrusion is more noticeable during bowel movements and tends to recede when she lies down. The patient reports that she feels a sense of incomplete bowel evacuation and has been experiencing some fecal leakage. Her medical history is significant for chronic constipation and multiple pregnancies.

*What is the most likely diagnosis of the patient?*

The most likely diagnosis for this patient is **Rectal Prolapse**. The symptoms of a protruding rectum, especially during bowel movements, a feeling of incomplete bowel evacuation, and fecal incontinence are indicative of rectal prolapse. The patient's history of chronic constipation and multiple pregnancies, which can weaken the pelvic floor muscles, are predisposing factors.
