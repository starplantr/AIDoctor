**Case 1:**

A 30-year-old woman presents to her primary care physician with persistent diarrhea, abdominal bloating, and recent unintentional weight loss. She also complains of a constant feeling of fatigue. She has a family history of celiac disease - her sister was diagnosed a few years ago. She also suffers from hypothyroidism.

*What is the most likely diagnosis of the patient?*

The most likely diagnosis for this patient is **Celiac Disease**. She presents with classic symptoms of persistent diarrhea, abdominal bloating, fatigue, and weight loss. The presence of a first-degree relative with celiac disease and a concurrent autoimmune condition (hypothyroidism) further increase her risk.

**Case 2:**

A 45-year-old man presents with chronic diarrhea and a rash that's been present for several weeks. He describes the rash as itchy and blistery, located primarily on his elbows and knees. He also mentions a history of type 1 diabetes.

*What is the most likely diagnosis of the patient?*

The most likely diagnosis for this patient is **Celiac Disease**. The patient's chronic diarrhea and rash (most likely dermatitis herpetiformis, a specific skin manifestation of celiac disease) point toward this diagnosis. Moreover, his type 1 diabetes, another autoimmune condition, increases his risk for celiac disease.
