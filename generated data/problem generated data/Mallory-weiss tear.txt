**Case 1:**

A 45-year-old man presents to the emergency department with a complaint of vomiting up blood. The patient says that he had been out drinking heavily the previous night and woke up with a severe hangover. He vomited multiple times throughout the day and then noticed that the vomit was becoming streaked with bright red blood. He also complained of moderate abdominal pain.

*What is the most likely diagnosis of the patient?*

The most likely diagnosis for this patient is a **Mallory-Weiss tear**. His history of heavy drinking and subsequent severe and prolonged vomiting leading to hematemesis (vomiting blood) is characteristic of a Mallory-Weiss tear. Chronic alcoholism is a significant risk factor for this condition, as it often leads to bouts of heavy vomiting that can cause these types of tears.
