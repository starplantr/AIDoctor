**Case 1:**

A 67-year-old woman presents to the emergency room with severe abdominal pain that she describes as cramping and episodic. She reports that she has not had a bowel movement for the past three days and has been unable to pass gas. She also mentions that she has vomited multiple times in the past 24 hours. Her past medical history is significant for a hysterectomy performed 20 years ago. On physical examination, her abdomen is noticeably distended and tender on palpation.

*What is the most likely diagnosis of the patient?*

The most likely diagnosis for this patient is **Intestinal Obstruction**. The patient's age, history of previous pelvic surgery (hysterectomy), severe cramping abdominal pain, constipation, inability to pass gas, and vomiting are classic signs and symptoms of intestinal obstruction. Her history of hysterectomy is notable as adhesions (scar tissue) from previous abdominal or pelvic surgery are the most common cause of intestinal obstruction.
