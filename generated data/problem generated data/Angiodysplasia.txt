**Case 1:**

A 70-year-old man presents to the clinic complaining of fatigue and shortness of breath for the past few weeks. He mentions passing dark, tarry stools occasionally. His past medical history is significant for aortic stenosis. Laboratory findings show low hemoglobin levels suggestive of anemia.

*What is the most likely diagnosis of the patient?*

The most likely diagnosis for this patient is **Angiodysplasia**. The patient's symptoms of fatigue and shortness of breath are consistent with anemia, which in this case could be due to chronic gastrointestinal bleeding. The presence of dark, tarry stools (melena) further suggests gastrointestinal bleeding. His past medical history of aortic stenosis is associated with an increased risk of gastrointestinal angiodysplasia.
