**Case 1:**

A 57-year-old man with a known history of alcoholic cirrhosis presents to the emergency department. He is extremely distressed and reports having vomited large amounts of blood. He also mentions passing black, tarry stools for the last day or so.

*What is the most likely diagnosis of the patient?*

The patient's history of alcoholic cirrhosis, along with his symptoms of vomiting blood and passing black, tarry stools, strongly suggests that he has **ruptured esophageal varices**. Esophageal varices commonly occur in people with cirrhosis and can rupture, causing severe bleeding. The black, tarry stools are due to the presence of digested blood, indicating a source of bleeding in the upper gastrointestinal tract.
