**Case 1:**

A 58-year-old obese man comes to the clinic complaining of recurrent chest pain that worsens after eating. He says the pain is often accompanied by a sour taste in his mouth, especially when he bends over or lies down after meals. The patient admits to having a history of heavy smoking and consuming alcohol regularly. 

*What is the most likely diagnosis of the patient?*

The most likely diagnosis for this patient is a **hiatal hernia**. This patient's age, obesity, and history of heavy smoking and alcohol use are risk factors for the condition. The symptoms of chest pain worsening after meals and the sour taste in the mouth (which indicates acid reflux) are common symptoms of a hiatal hernia. This condition occurs when the stomach pushes upward through the diaphragm, often causing acid reflux, especially when the patient bends over or lies down after meals.
