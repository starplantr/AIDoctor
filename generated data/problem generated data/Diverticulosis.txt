**Case 1:**

A 68-year-old woman presents to your clinic with complaints of bloating and mild abdominal cramps over the past several weeks. She reports a history of chronic constipation. She follows a diet that is low in fiber and high in processed foods. The patient is also a current smoker and has been smoking for the past 30 years.

*What is the most likely diagnosis of the patient?*

Given her age, symptoms of bloating and abdominal cramps, history of chronic constipation, a diet low in fiber, and her smoking history, the most likely diagnosis is **Diverticulosis**.
