**Case 1:**

A 17-year-old boy presents to the emergency room with severe abdominal pain that started around his navel but has since migrated to his lower right abdomen. He reports feeling nauseated and hasn't been able to eat since the pain started. His temperature is measured at 101 degrees Fahrenheit. He has no significant past medical history.

*What is the most likely diagnosis of the patient?*

The most likely diagnosis for this patient is **appendicitis**. The patient's symptoms - including the progression of the pain from the umbilical area to the lower right abdomen, anorexia, nausea, and fever - are classic presentations of appendicitis. Appendicitis is common in this age group and can progress rapidly, so immediate surgical consultation for appendectomy is warranted.
